Kandla Grey Sandstone: The Complete Guide to Applications, Benefits, Finishes and Specifications

Kandla Grey Sandstone • Paving • Pool Copings • Curbs • Pier Caps • Walling

Kandla Grey Sandstone is one of the most specified natural paving stones for architects, designers, contractors, and procurement teams working across residential, hospitality, public realm, and commercial landscapes. Its cool silver-grey palette, practical slip resistance, and high outdoor durability make it a dependable stone for both statement projects and high-traffic applications.

Processed in Rajasthan, India, Kandla Grey combines visual consistency with fabrication flexibility. It is regularly supplied as paving, calibrated slabs, steps, wall copings, pool copings, curbs, and pier caps, helping project teams maintain one coherent material language across entire site packages.

What Is Kandla Grey Sandstone?

Kandla Grey is a natural sandstone with tones ranging from pale silver and dove grey to blue-grey and occasional warm undertones. Like many quartz-rich sandstones, it offers a balance of workability and structural strength, which is why it remains widely used in global paving and hardscape projects.

From broader sandstone references and hardscape industry practices, buyers prioritize stones that combine grain stability, weather tolerance, and finish versatility. Kandla Grey performs strongly on all three fronts when supplied and installed correctly.

Surface Finishes Available

Kandla Grey can be processed in multiple textures depending on the balance required between visual softness and anti-slip performance.

Technical and Project Planning Considerations

  • Sub-base and bedding quality: Long-term performance depends heavily on base preparation, drainage planning, and consistent bedding.
  • Installation detailing: Joint width, laying pattern, and edge restraint design should align with project load conditions.
  • Sealing strategy: Sealing is optional but often recommended for easier maintenance and improved stain resistance in high-use areas.
  • Batch management: For large projects, mock-up approvals and phased dispatch planning improve visual continuity.
  • Climate-fit usage: Kandla Grey is used in varied climates globally; final detailing should still align with local site conditions and standards.
